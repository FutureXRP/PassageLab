Big Idea
Because Christ has fully satisfied God's condemnation, believers walk not under the law of sin and death but under the liberating law of the Spirit who gives life.
Key Word — Greek
κατάκριμα
katakrima · G2631

Not merely guilt, but the executed sentence — the actual penalty falling on the condemned. Paul's point: that verdict has been fully discharged in Christ.
Historical Context
Roman courts condemned with finality — no appeal for the lower classes. Paul's readers would feel the weight of no condemnation viscerally. Acquittal was resurrection language.
Archaeology — Recent Finding
Excavations beneath the Mamertine Prison (2023) uncovered Roman-era legal documents confirming the permanence of capital condemnation orders — illuminating exactly the legal imagery Paul invokes in verse 1.
